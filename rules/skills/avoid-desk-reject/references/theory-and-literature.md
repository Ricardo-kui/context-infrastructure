# Theory and Literature

## Use This Module When

- The user asks about theory, literature review, hypotheses, or mechanism
- The manuscript cites many papers but the contribution still feels unanchored
- The issue is whether the paper clearly enters an existing conversation

## What Editors Are Screening For

- Whether the literature review frames the research question rather than merely summarizing papers
- Whether theory is distinguishable from literature review
- Whether the mechanism is explicit, not implied
- Whether hypotheses are clear, directional when needed, and falsifiable
- Whether alternative explanations are surfaced and bounded

## Common Desk-Reject Triggers

- A citation dump with no clear conversation or gap
- Literature review that does not lead to the question or hypotheses
- Theoretical contribution asserted but not mechanism-based
- Hypotheses that are absent, vague, or bundled across multiple relationships
- Empirical regularities being sold as theory
- A journal-specific conversation being ignored in favor of generic citations

## Questions to Answer

- What exact conversation is the paper entering and extending?
- What mechanism links the constructs?
- Are the hypotheses testing theoretical relations or only restating empirical patterns?
- Could the hypotheses be wrong in a meaningful way?
- What competing explanation would a skeptical reviewer immediately raise?

## Safe Outputs for the Preflight Report

- Use `high risk` when the literature review does not position the manuscript in a recognizable conversation
- Do not let "more citations" substitute for "clearer mechanism"
- If the theory problem is foundational, route toward restructuring before polishing prose
