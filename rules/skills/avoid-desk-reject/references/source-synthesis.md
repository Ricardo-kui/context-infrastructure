# Source Synthesis

## Sources Used for V1

1. Beugelsdijk and Bird on avoiding desk rejects in JIBS
2. Craig on desk rejection in Family Business Review
3. Edmans on learnings from 1000 rejections in Review of Finance

## Shared Editorial Logic

Across all three sources, editors are screening for the same core question: is this manuscript worth sending forward, given limited editorial and reviewer attention?

The shared logic can be compressed into five filters:

1. **Fit**
   - Does the paper belong in this journal's conversation?
   - Is the topic framed for this journal's readers, not just for the author's subfield?
2. **Contribution**
   - Does the paper move the reader's prior, rather than add a small wrinkle?
   - Is the question important enough for the outlet?
3. **Communication / Exposition**
   - Can an editor see the contribution quickly from the abstract and opening pages?
   - Is the writing clear enough for a reviewer to help rather than decode?
4. **Rigor / Execution**
   - Do theory, hypotheses, measures, design, and claims line up?
   - Is the evidence strong enough for the language used?
5. **Maturity / Transparency**
   - Does the submission package signal that the manuscript is ready for review rather than still undercooked?

## What Each Source Adds

| Source | Primary lens | What V1 should borrow |
| --- | --- | --- |
| JIBS editorial | Communication + rigor | Story discipline, audience awareness, abstract quality, theory-method linkage, construct-measure alignment |
| FBR editorial | Early-screen heuristics | Abstract, first three paragraphs, literature review as fit signal, underdeveloped submissions, external feedback before submission |

## Default V1 Positioning

- Default standard: **English management / international business top-journal desk review**
- Finance evidence is **supplementary**, not co-equal
- The skill should diagnose and route; it should not auto-rewrite by default
- The first question is not "Can this be polished?" but "Should this be submitted here in the current form?"

## Source-Specific Cautions

- JIBS and FBR are management-facing sources, so they are highly relevant for journal fit, theory, and introduction structure in management research.
- Edmans is finance-facing, so V1 should use it mainly to sharpen contribution, execution, and exposition standards, not to redefine the baseline outlet logic.
- Some source-specific heuristics are too local to become axioms. Keep journal-specific details inside the module references, not in the global rules layer.
