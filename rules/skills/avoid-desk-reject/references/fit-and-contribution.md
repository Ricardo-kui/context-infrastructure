# Fit and Contribution

## Use This Module When

- The user asks about target journal fit
- The user wants a desk-reject diagnosis for a full paper or extended abstract
- The paper may be technically competent but still feels minor, narrow, or mispositioned

## What Editors Are Screening For

- Whether the paper belongs in the journal's main conversation
- Whether the question matters to the journal's typical reader
- Whether the contribution changes a prior belief or materially upgrades the evidence
- Whether the paper travels beyond a narrow setting
- Whether the paper is better suited to a different outlet

## Common Desk-Reject Triggers

- The paper fits another journal better than the stated target journal
- The result is first, but still predictable or low-stakes
- The paper sells a setting, sample, or method as the main story without theoretical payoff
- The context is narrow and the manuscript never explains transportability
- The paper adds a wrinkle, not a reweighting of what readers should believe

## Questions to Answer

- What journal conversation is this manuscript entering?
- Why should this journal's readers care now?
- What prior belief should change if the paper is correct?
- Is the novelty about the question, mechanism, evidence, or merely the setting?
- If the paper disappeared, what would the literature genuinely lose?

## Safe Outputs for the Preflight Report

- Use `stop and reposition` when fit or importance clearly fails
- Use `major revision before submit` when the question may matter but the contribution is underspecified
- Do not lead with sentence-level polishing if the true bottleneck is fit or contribution
