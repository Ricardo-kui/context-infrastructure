# Empirical Credibility

## Use This Module When

- The user asks about methods, identification, measurement, or empirical credibility
- The paper makes strong claims that may outrun the design
- The manuscript may be vulnerable on construct-measure alignment or causal language

## What Editors Are Screening For

- Whether the evidence supports the language of the claims
- Whether measures are aligned with the underlying constructs
- Whether the identification strategy is visible and credible
- Whether the sample and empirical setting are appropriate for the stated question
- Whether the manuscript acknowledges what it cannot establish

## Common Desk-Reject Triggers

- Causal claims resting on correlational evidence
- Key constructs being proxied distally without discussion
- Hypotheses, measures, and estimands not lining up
- Identification strategy hidden, under-explained, or weaker than the prose suggests
- General conclusions drawn from a single narrow case without justification
- Robustness or limitations treated as an afterthought when they are central to credibility

## Questions to Answer

- What is the strongest defensible claim: association, mechanism-consistent evidence, or causal effect?
- Does each key measure match the theoretical construct closely enough for the claim made?
- What assumptions must hold for the paper's headline claim?
- What sample or setting limitations should already be acknowledged in the main text?
- If a skeptical reviewer read only the design section, what would they distrust first?

## Safe Outputs for the Preflight Report

- Downgrade the verdict when the paper's language outruns the design
- If the design only supports correlation, say so explicitly
- When the key risk is empirical credibility, route to method or code skills before stylistic cleanup
